Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

Description

Spaghetti Aglio e Olio can be made with pantry staples - spaghetti, oil, garlic, red chilli flakes, black pepper and fresh parsley. In the repertoire of traditional Italian pasta dishes, there is none as simple and straightforward as Spaghetti aglio e olio. Its literal translation is garlic and olive oil which is really the essence of this dish which originates in Naples.

While being simple, no two aglio e olio are alike. It takes practice to make aglio e olio perfectly as the trick is about toasting the garlic slices to a perfect golden brown in the olive oil. And if you feel like wanting to give it your own kick, the options are endless.

Don't be afraid, if patient no-one make aglio e olio wrong.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pount uncooked spaghetti
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • ¼ teaspon red pepper flakes, or to taste
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh italian parsley
  • 1 cup finely grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese

Directions

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring occasionally until cooked through but firm to the bite, about 10 to 12 minutes. Drain and transfer to pasta bowl.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, combine olive oil and garlic in a cold skillet. Cook over medium heat to slowly toast garlic, about 10 minutes. Reduce heat to medium-low when olive oil begins to bubble. Cook and stir until garlic is golden brown, about another 5 minutes. Remove from heat.
  3. Stir red pepper flakes, salt and black pepper into pasta. Pour in hot olive oil and garlic, sprinkle on Italian parsley and half od the Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese; toss until combined.
  4. Serve pasta topped with the remaining Parmigiano-reggiano cheese.
